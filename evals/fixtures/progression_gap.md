Arin spent the day in town. He bought bread, spoke with three merchants, watched the rain gather in the gutters, and returned to the inn before sunset. Nothing changed. He slept badly and woke to another grey morning.

At noon he sat by the window and considered the same problem he had considered yesterday. The answer did not alter, his position did not alter, and the people around him did not force a decision. He waited until evening because waiting was easier than risking a mistake.

The chapter ended there.
