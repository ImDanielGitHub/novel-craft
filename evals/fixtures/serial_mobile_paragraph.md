The corridor went on and on beneath the arena, its walls sweating with old rain and old blood, and Kade kept walking even though the torch had burned down to a thumb of blue flame and even though every sensible part of him knew that the trial marshal should have called the match over after the third bell, but the system had not spoken, the rival had not conceded, the crowd above had not stopped chanting, and the wound in his side had begun to pulse in time with the sigil on his wrist, which meant either the dungeon had accepted him as a candidate or the poison had reached his core, and he could not decide which possibility frightened him more because both meant the same thing: the next door would open only for someone willing to pay.
